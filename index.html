Revenue Growth Trajectory

The Philippine BPO industry consistently outperforms global benchmarks. Revenue is projected to reach $40 billion in 2025, with an ambitious target of $59 billion by 2028, showcasing sustained investor confidence and market demand.

Employment Expansion

The sector is a major job creator, with employment expected to hit 1.9 million in 2025. The IBPAP roadmap aims for an aggressive target of 2.5 million jobs by 2028, reinforcing its role as a key pillar of the national workforce.

Strategic Shift: From BPO to KPO

The industry is evolving beyond traditional call centers, moving up the value chain into Knowledge Process Outsourcing (KPO). This strategic pivot leverages the Philippines' highly skilled talent pool for more complex, specialized services that global clients demand.

Enduring Competitive Advantages

The Philippines maintains its global leadership through a unique combination of talent, cost-effectiveness, and a supportive ecosystem. These core strengths make it a resilient and attractive destination for outsourcing.

Adapting to AI: Job Transformation

Instead of displacing jobs, AI is transforming them. The industry is focused on upskilling its workforce to collaborate with AI, handling more complex tasks and creating new, higher-value roles. This chart visualizes the expected impact on job roles.

Philippine MSMEs: The Backbone of the Economy (2023 Data)

Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) form the vast majority of business establishments in the Philippines, playing a crucial role in job creation and economic activity across various local government units (LGUs).

MSME Distribution by Enterprise Size

Of the 1.25 million business establishments in 2023, 99.63% were MSMEs, with micro enterprises making up the largest share.

Employment Generated by MSMEs (2023)

IBPAP Industry Roadmap 2028

A clear strategic vision guides the industry's future. The roadmap outlines key phases of development, from building foundational strengths to achieving global leadership in high-value digital services.

Phase 1: Strengthening the Core (2023-2024)

Focus on solidifying existing services, enhancing workforce skills through upskilling programs, and leveraging government support like the CREATE MORE Act to boost competitiveness.

Phase 2: Accelerating Growth (2025-2026)

Driving expansion into higher-value KPO services, increasing adoption of AI and automation, and promoting geographic diversification into countryside locations to tap new talent pools.

Phase 3: Achieving Global Leadership (2027-2028)

Aiming to capture a larger share of the global KPO market, establishing the Philippines as a hub for innovation and attracting more Global Capability Centers (GCCs) for strategic functions.

The Goal: $59 Billion Revenue & 2.5 Million Jobs

The roadmap culminates in ambitious targets that will solidify the Philippines' status as a premier digital services hub on the world stage.
